Week 7 - Cultural Blog

Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

When I think of the time in my life that I’ve been the proudest, the following terms come to mind: accomplishment, pleasure, and intellectual status.

The above terms that I’ve associated with the time I’ve felt most proud in my life are not values and I do not try to live up to them. The feelings of accomplishment, pleasure, and intellectual status were evoked in me because I believed what I did was something to be proud of and was valuable. These feelings came from the idea that I was more valuable as a result of achieving some goal that I was proud of. This is more closely related to vanity than any coherent system of values.

The last topic that someone asked me for my advice was on the subject of communicating to other people.

When I think about my values, I tend to feel righteous, i.e. morally right or justifiable (not self-righteous). I do think my values can help mediate stereotype threat because empathizing with individuals allows me to be considerate and sensitive to the needs of those individuals. Just as I wouldn’t want to be judged based on a stereotype that I had nothing to do with creating, I will not subject another individual to that sort of treatment.

Some exercises that will help me during DBC while feeling down at DBC are mindfulness and self-empathy.

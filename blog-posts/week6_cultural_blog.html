Week 6 - Cultural Blog

The Stereotype Threat

The stereotype threat is when group members act in such a way as to conform to certain expectations placed on their group by individuals who are typically outside their group.

I can’t say that I’ve ever felt that I’ve been the victim of a stereotype threat. The idea makes a lot of sense to me and I can imagine many people being the victim of a stereotype threat.

This exercise has many questions based on how I’ve felt in a situation where I’ve been treated in such a way. Since I don’t feel that I have, the exercise goes on to say:

If you don't think you've ever felt it, can you imagine some groups that might?

Within the above question, lies the very problem stereotype threat comes from. The term “group” is nothing more than a label. Groups can’t think and it can’t feel because it’s not real. Individuals are real, living, biological organisms that can think and feel.

In order to combat the stereotype threat, we need to stop stereotyping individuals as members of groups, and instead value them for their own individual characteristics and attributes.
